My research in resource allocation centers on the design and analysis of optimal priority rules for dynamic systems. I specialize in the Gittins index and Whittle index for multi-armed bandits, particularly for applications in telecommunications, sensor networks, and stochastic scheduling.

<section id="expertise" markdown="1">
## Methodological Expertise
* **Restless Multi-Armed Bandits (RMAB):** Development of marginal productivity index (Whittle index) policies for complex priority allocation.
* **Stochastic Scheduling:** Optimization of systems with abandonments, delays, and heterogeneous service requirements.
* **Performance Evaluation:** Utilizing Markov Decision Processes (MDP) and fluid limits to analyze large-scale systems.
</section>

<section id="software" markdown="1">
## Software Packages
* **BinaryBandit (2019):** A Julia package and R Shiny App for evaluating finite-horizon bandit problems and index policies.
* **Whittle (2012):** Software for computing Whittle indices for various restless bandit models.
</section>

<section id="papers" markdown="1">
## Key Publications
* **Information-Freshness-Aware Resource Allocation:** Joint optimization of Age of Information (AoI) and energy in sensor networks. *IEEE Transactions on Communications* (2025).
* **Strategic Interactions in Restless Bandits:** Analysis of competitive behavior in dynamic priority systems. *Operations Research* (2023).
* **Whittle Index for Abandonment:** Optimal control of queues where users or tasks may leave the system. *Mathematics of Operations Research* (2018).
* **Index Policies for Multiclass Forests:** Generalizing index rules for complex branching processes. *SIAM Journal on Control and Optimization* (2011).
